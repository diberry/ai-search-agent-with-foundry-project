To locate lava at night, thermal imaging satellites such as Landsat 8 or VIIRS (Visible Infrared Imaging Radiometer Suite) can be used
 These instruments capture the infrared glow of lava flows, distinguishing hot lava from cooler surroundings
 For example, during eruptions, glowing lava appears bright white or red in these images, allowing scientists to monitor active volcanic sites remotely
 Additionally, moonlight and satellite thermal sensors can enhance visibility of volcanic activity, even through clouds or ash plumes
 Monitoring tools like these are especially helpful for tracking hazards and ensuring public safety
